\chapternotnumbered{Summary and Outlook}

In \Cref{chap:FLOWS}, we described the problem of our interest: flows over topography. Orographic flows influence internal gravity waves, clear-air turbulence and eg downlospe windstorms - they are important. But at the same time, they are tough to predict --- i wanted to help with that by using SPH.

In \Cref{chap:MODELLING} we discussed some specifics of these flows, for example the boundary conditions. Most importantly, well balanced schemse were introduced.

\Cref{chap:SPH} is devoted to the presentation of the main method used. The governing equations have been derived classicaly, then Hamiltonian, then Lagrangian. a new density independent formulation has been proposed and an addition has been added -- THAT IS CONTRIBUTION. Morevoer, well balanced SPH methods have been dicusedd - THAT IS NEW

\Cref{chap:EXPERIMENTS} showcased the methods. My WB method was the best. Some success with the simulations, but tensile instability ruined it.
All in all, it seems SPH could be kind of useful, but it requires the deveolpment of serious new advanced techniques - eg well balanced density independent \delta sph formulation.
